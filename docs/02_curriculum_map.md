# Curriculum Map

## Purpose of This Document
This document defines the overall structure of the curriculum for Math Journey. It is the master map of the learning path. It should show how the full journey is broken into major worlds, what each world covers, and how learners progress from basic arithmetic to advanced high school and early college topics.

This is a strategic curriculum document, not a fully detailed syllabus. Chapter-level detail will be defined elsewhere.

## Curriculum Design Goals
The curriculum should:
- begin at true beginner level
- progress in a clear and coherent sequence
- remain historically and culturally rich
- support mathematical rigor
- connect mathematics to physics and the real world
- scale into a long-form learning platform
- support both narrative flow and prerequisite logic

## Two Parallel Structures
The curriculum operates on two parallel structures:

### 1. Learning Path
This is the skill-based sequence learners follow for mastery.

### 2. History Path
This is the cultural and historical layer that shows where ideas emerged, evolved, and were transmitted.

These two paths should inform each other, but they are not identical. The order of mathematical dependency does not always match strict historical chronology.

## Overall Learning Arc
The platform begins with:
- number sense
- arithmetic
- fractions
- ratios
- basic mathematical notation

It then builds through:
- geometry
- algebra
- coordinate thinking
- functions
- trigonometry
- pre-calculus ideas
- calculus foundations
- probability and statistics foundations
- vectors, fields, and introductory physics
- special relativity as a modern capstone

## World Structure
The curriculum is organized into eight worlds.

---

## World 00 — The Language of Number

### Purpose
Introduce mathematics as a human invention and build a solid foundation in number, arithmetic, fractions, ratio, and notation.

### Why It Matters
This world provides the base for everything that follows. Weaknesses here will undermine algebra, geometry, and physics later.

### Core Topics
- why humans invented number
- counting systems
- place value
- zero
- addition and subtraction
- multiplication and division
- fractions
- decimals
- percentages
- negative numbers
- estimation
- order of operations
- basic notation

### Historical and Cultural Threads
- early counting and tally systems
- Mesopotamia
- accounting and trade
- India and the development of zero
- number systems across cultures

### Candidate Chapters
1. Why Numbers Exist
2. Counting and Early Number Systems
3. Place Value
4. Zero
5. Addition and Subtraction as Structure
6. Multiplication as Groups and Area
7. Division as Sharing and Rate
8. Fractions
9. Decimals
10. Percentages
11. Negative Numbers
12. Order of Operations and Notation

### Exit Competencies
By the end of this world, the learner should be able to:
- compute confidently with whole numbers
- understand place value
- use fractions and decimals
- convert simple percentages
- reason about negative numbers
- read basic mathematical notation

## Curriculum Progression Logic

### Foundational Dependency Order
The approximate dependency chain is:

1. Number sense and arithmetic
2. Fractions, decimals, ratios, and notation
3. Geometry and proof foundations
4. Algebra
5. Graphs and functions
6. Trigonometry
7. Calculus foundations
8. Probability and statistics foundations
9. Vectors and introductory physics
10. Relativity as capstone content

### Important Notes
- Historical content may loop backward or sideways even when skill progression moves forward.
- Some side quests or optional essays may appear outside strict skill order.
- Not every learner must complete every enrichment branch to continue core progression.

## Content Types Within the Curriculum
Each world may eventually include several content types:

### Core Chapters
Required for progression.

### Side Quests
Optional cultural, historical, biographical, or conceptual deep dives.

### Proof Rooms
Focused reasoning and proof content for learners who want more formal depth.

### Labs
Interactive explorations, simulations, or applied investigations.

### Reviews
Cumulative chapter sets for consolidation and spaced repetition.

## Recommended First Build Scope
The full curriculum is large. The first implementation should not attempt to write it all.

The first build should focus on:
- finalizing the world structure
- defining chapter schema
- identifying the first 6 to 12 chapters
- creating one complete sample chapter
- testing the narrative-rigor-practice model
